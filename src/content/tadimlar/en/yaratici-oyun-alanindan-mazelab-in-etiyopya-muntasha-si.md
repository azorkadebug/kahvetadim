On this page we're stopping by Prague once more. This time the name behind the cup is Mazelab — a team that has made a name for itself on the city's coffee scene both behind the counter and at the competition table. The story of founders Jackie and Thu actually begins in a coffee shop too; the two meet there, and then go on to build both their life and their work together.

Jackie's background is in design and photography. The idea for Mazelab was born on a trip to Iceland: to combine a coffee shop with a photo gallery. Although their first café made its name with latte art, Jackie realized the work doesn't end with aesthetics — that the real depth lies in taste, aroma and that whole sensory journey. That's why they describe Mazelab as a kind of "creative playground" — a place that seats coffee at the same table as art.

They got into roasting in May 2023, after clocking six years in the industry. Their intention isn't to chase what's rare or expensive; in their own words, it's to hunt down beans that "offer hidden, distinctive flavors of their own." That approach sits well with this page, which cares about transparency and terroir. A little note for those wondering how serious the work is: the 2024 Czech Brewers Cup title was Jackie's, and the 2025 World Brewers championship was won with Mazelab's SOLO Dripper. So there's quite a body of experience behind the cup. Let's move on to the coffee's profile.

🌱 **Coffee Profile**

- **Origin:** Ethiopia
- **Region:** Arbegona, Sidama
- **Producer:** Muntasha Family
- **Variety:** JARC 74158
- **Process:** Natural
- **Altitude:** 2400 m
- **Roast level:** Light – Light/Medium
- **Roasting machine:** Probat 12
- **Roaster:** Mazelab

I'd never come across a roaster who includes a detail like the roasting machine before; a very thoughtful touch. Since the roaster doesn't provide a specific recipe, I work at it, and add the recipe we liked best to our site.

⚙️ **Brew Recipe & Equipment**

For this natural Ethiopia that doesn't like high heat, my favorite was to stay at 92 degrees; when I raised the temperature, bitter notes pushed the coffee away from me. A salute to the classic water-holding Ethiopian naturals — this coffee of ours is one of them too.

From our coffee shelf I'm picking our trusty old Origami Air S dripper. My filter paper choice is th3, as abaca plus turned out too slow and abaca too fast.

🌡️ **Water Temperature:** 92°C

💧 **Bloom:** 30 seconds

🌊 **Pours:** 4 stages (50/100/50/50)

⏱️ **Total Time:** 2:25-2:30

**On the Nose (Aroma)**

The coffee has a very colorful, very enjoyable nose — we can't get enough of smelling it. Tropical aromas draw us in toward the coffee.

**On the Palate**

A coffee you almost chew on, this Muntasha; warm, it brings to mind the astringency of orange. Within 2-3 minutes, red fruits, yellow plum and citrus — a complex coffee in every sip. It's anything but monotonous; with every tasting there are different layers, and the transitions captivate us. A sweetish, flavorful coffee with medium–medium-low acidity.

**Final Word**

Mazelab has been the roaster that's impressed me most lately; I hope one day I'll get the chance to experience it in person. I'm certain their other coffees will find a place on our site. May your cup be shared with your loved ones, and may your sips always be a pleasure.
