To kick off this journey, which we began by telling the stories behind coffees, I'd love to introduce you to our very first guest. When I heard a dear friend of mine was heading to Thessaloniki, I figured it was the perfect chance to finally try the **Hue** selection that had been on my radar for a while. After a short wait, the coffee finally made it into my hands and took its place on the counter.

**Brew Recipe & Process**

Staying true to the roaster's recommendation, I went with the **Orea V4 (Classic Base)** and a **Kalita** filter. Even though only 10 days had passed since the roast date, I could tell the degassing had progressed remarkably well.

- **Water Temperature:** 91°C
- **Bloom:** 40 seconds
- **Pour:** 4-pour
- **Total Brew Time:** 2:10

**On the Nose (Aroma)**

In the dry fragrance, those characteristic fermented notes brought on by the thermal shock process were front and center. But the moment the coffee met the water, that fermented quality vanished completely and gave way to what felt like an entire flower garden. While the roaster's product description highlights "orange blossom," to me this is a far richer, multi-layered floral bouquet that simply can't be pinned down to a single flower. With the sheer joy of not having brewed a coffee this captivating in a long time, I dive straight into the tasting.

**On the Palate (Flavor Profile)**

From the very first sip, that signature **Gesha elegance** announces itself. A lively, bright acidity greets you on the palate; but this is by no means a tiring or off-putting acidity. The coffee holds onto its sweetness from start to finish, and this is certainly no ordinary sweetness, but rather a structure full of character. The journey across the palate continues with citrus flavors dancing in harmony with those floral notes from the nose. And in the finish, with its body and long-lasting persistence, it proves in every sense just how high-quality a cup it is.

**Final Word**

We close out the first piece in our series with a layered, complex coffee whose processing has been handled with real skill. This bean is absolutely not one of those monotonous, "boring" Geshas. It offers a textbook profile that you could point to directly as a reference for any coffee lover wondering, *"Why is the Gesha variety so prized?"*
