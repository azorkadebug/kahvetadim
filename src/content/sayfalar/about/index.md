title: The craft that fits in a cup
---
We see coffee not merely as a beverage to be consumed, but as a living agricultural product. On farms at the far end of the world, on high-altitude slopes, we look for the reflection in the cup of the months of patience of people whose hands meet the soil.

At the heart of our tasting journey lies a deep respect for that hard agricultural labor and the sweat poured into coffee farms. When we search for the right water temperature, calculate grind size down to the millimeter, or choose a brewing method, we have a single aim: to reveal the land's characteristic qualities (terroir) in their most transparent form, without wasting the labor the farmer packed into that bean.

This is exactly why we move away from flavors that have grown ordinary and chase quality coffee instead. Here, we examine beans that do justice to fermentation processes, carefully selected varieties, and proper roasting.
